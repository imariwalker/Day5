Teaching Statement

Diversity Teaching Statement

At an early age, I discovered a desire for disseminating knowledge in a way that is relatable to my peers and younger age groups. Through their own passion, faculty mentors and teachers have inspired me to contribute to teaching others and furthering my research on plastic additives in the environment.

Passion for Teaching

From my experience as a tutor and a mentor for undergraduate students in STEM, it has provided me fulfillment in teaching others the joy of learning. My passion stems from the direct impact that I can have on the next generation of students studying science and engineering. Engaging with these students has shown me that many have the potential to go on to graduate studies and do important research in the field of stem.

Teaching Objectives

My goal in teaching is to encourage students to approach real world problems critically and creatively evaluate new solutions. I plan to push students to identify problems, use their background knowledge to formulate a hypothesis, and utilize new technology to come to a solution.

Teaching Experience

As a student, instructors have inspired my passion for science through their own enthusiasm, availability, approachability, and outgoingness. Therefore, as a professor, I aim to utilize these characteristics in my methods of teaching. This will be completed by having multiple scheduled office hours every week, having an open door policy, answering students emails promptly, and personally reaching out to students that are struggling in my class. This will encourage an atmosphere that is open and inviting for students that have different methods for learning and reaching out for help. I believe instituting anonymous 1 minute reflections once a week and doodle polls on the course content will also be important to get a feel for which subjects were not explained well for the class. I will also have technology like piazza that allows for students to anonymously post questions and answers for concepts that may need a different perspective to clarify. Two challenges that I will face in teaching is working with students that are less attentive and managing time to efficiently teach the important topics. To fix that I believe that having patience, creativity and great organization will be vital. To truly determine how my teaching has impacted student learning, final exams and projects will demonstrate the students ability to problem solve and communicating their steps to coming to conclusions. I believe that for the field of stem, it is important to put into practice the theory and concepts that are being taught by putting together interactive experiments, demonstrations, and videos.

As a mentor for undergraduate students, my method of creating an open learning environment is by openly listening to their needs, their struggles, and their passions. As a Sensor Saturday Academy mentor for 8th grade students, I assist students in learning concepts of linear algebra, circuits, water quality, temperature. After each powerpoint lecture, I walk through the assigned worksheets with groups of 2-3 students to reinforce the concept. In the afternoons of the Sensor Academy, I assist the students through the 5 building blocks of engineering design. Each week they are designated a task to complete, a list of material they can use, and a time frame to complete it in. My job is to guide a small group’s school of thought into creating the most cost and time effective engineering solution. It is incredibly rewarding to see their joy of learning how to accomplish these task through trial and error. My teaching method in these groups is to encourage each person in the group to communicate their opinions and ideas on solving this problem to make the space inclusive and encourage team work. Each week, I also flip each person’s role in the group to allow each one learn how to take on a variety of leadership positions.

Ongoing Development

The upcoming year, I will have the opportunity to become a teaching assistant for environmental chemistry. This will allow me to further improve as an instructor and develop my teaching abilities for the future. I believe that mentoring is also an important part of becoming a better teacher. Understanding the background that students come from allows for the class to be better tailored for each group and allows for a greater diversity of thought. As a professor, I plan to continue to mentor underrepresented high school and undergraduate students through participating in underrepresented minority research experiences. I would also like to be a faculty advisor for the university chapter of national society of black engineers. My teaching experiences as a mentor for undergraduate students and as a peer tutor have encourage me to further my training in teaching in preparation of becoming a future faculty instructor. I am working to a certificate in college teaching at Duke University, to further improve my teaching skills and learn the latest instructional technology and methods for class design.
